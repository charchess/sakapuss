## Overview

**Page Purpose:** Chronological feed of all logged entries — the window into Camille's care history. When she notices Pixel eating less, or when the vet asks "since when?", this page replaces uncertainty with facts. Filterable by category to surface what matters now.

**User Situation:** Camille is slightly worried. Pixel has been eating less, or the vet just asked "since when has he lost weight?" She opens the app to check the data. She needs to see patterns, not just individual events.

**Success Criteria:**
- Most recent entries visible without scrolling
- Category filter accessible in 1 tap
- Weight entry tap leads directly to weight chart (04.2)
- Anomalies highlighted proactively — they come to Camille, she doesn't search for them

**Entry Points:**
- Bottom navigation "Timeline" tab (primary)
- Animal profile "See history" link
- Dashboard recent activity "See all" link

**Exit Points:**
- Tap weight entry → 04.2 Graphique de poids
- Tap animal avatar → 04.3 Profil animal
- Bottom nav → Home, Reminders, Profile

## Technical Notes

- **Valeur Historique Progressive:** The timeline naturally demonstrates increasing value over time. Weeks 1-4: recent trends visible. Months 2-6: emerging correlations. 12+ months: seasonal patterns. The UI does not need to change — the data density itself tells the story.
- **Performance:** Initial load fetches last 7 days. Infinite scroll loads 20 entries at a time. Virtualized list for smooth scrolling on large histories.
- **Anomaly detection:** Server-side analysis compares recent weight entries against rolling average. Threshold: >5% change in 2 weeks triggers banner. Algorithm refined over time.
- **Filter persistence:** Active filter is remembered in session (not across app restarts). Animal filter persists within the session.
- **Sticky filter bar:** Uses CSS `position: sticky` with `z-index` above feed content. Background must be opaque (`color-bg`) to prevent content bleed-through on scroll.
- **Offline:** All entries cached via service worker. New entries logged offline appear in feed immediately and sync when reconnected.
- **Grouping logic:** Entries grouped by calendar day in user's timezone. "Today" and "Yesterday" use relative labels; older dates show day name + date.
